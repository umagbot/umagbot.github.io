Pushing the limits of agricultural robotics in Manitoba


The University of Manitoba Agricultural Robotics team is a student-run group committed to the development of innovative, autonomous agricultural machines that meet the needs of the future farmer.

A proud participant in the agBOT challenge

ENGINEERING FOR THE FUTURE FARMER

The UM-agBOT team is dedicated to driving innovation in the agricultural technology field. To that end, the team will participate in the annual agBOT Challenge organized by Gerrish Farms in Rockville, Indiana, starting in May, 2019. The challenge aims to improve agricultural robotic systems in key areas including selective weed and feed and soil sampling, which form the two divisions of our team. Participating in the agBOT Challenge allows us to take part in redefining the limits of agricultural robotics.

Our team is composed of the following subsystems


Machine Vision
Navigation
Actuation

Our members' motivations range from fighting climate change through advanced input management, reducing drudgery of repetitive or dangerous agricultural tasks, adding value to farming practices, or advancing their skills in embedded systems and machine learning.
